# 1. Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) defines the process for monitoring, reviewing and managing operational and security logs generated by the Blowbits Solutions LLP GST Suvidha Provider (GSP) platform.

The objective of this procedure is to ensure that operational events, security events and system activities are monitored to support platform security, operational stability, troubleshooting and audit requirements.

# 2. Scope

This procedure applies to operational logs, application logs, infrastructure logs, security logs and audit records associated with the Blowbits Solutions LLP GST Suvidha Provider (GSP) platform.

Operational records and supporting evidence generated during monitoring activities are maintained separately.

# 3. Roles & Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
| ---- | -------------- |
| Platform Operations Lead | Monitor operational events, maintain logging configuration and support investigation of system events. |
| Information Security Lead | Review security events, access-related events and monitoring observations with potential security impact. |
| GSP Governance Lead | Review significant monitoring observations, exceptions or audit-relevant matters where required. |

# 4. Log Monitoring Procedure

## Step 1 – Log Collection

Operational and security events generated by the GSP platform shall be collected through approved logging mechanisms.

Collected events may include:

- Application and API events.
- Authentication and access events.
- Administrative and privileged activity events.
- Infrastructure, operating environment and cloud service events.
- Database and data service events where applicable.
- Network, firewall, security group and connectivity events where available.
- Backup, replication and recovery events.
- Security events.
- Operational alerts.

Log collection shall use AWS-managed time synchronisation where available to support reliable timestamps and correlation across systems.

Logging configurations should avoid recording secrets, credentials, tokens or sensitive taxpayer information where practicable. Where logs may contain sensitive information, access shall be restricted to authorised personnel.

## Step 2 – Monitoring

Collected logs shall be monitored to identify operational abnormalities, security events and system anomalies.

Monitoring activities may include:

- Service availability.
- Authentication failures.
- Administrative activities.
- Infrastructure health.
- Resource utilisation.
- Security alerts.

Operational monitoring shall be performed as part of regular platform operations. Security, access and audit events shall be reviewed at least monthly by the Information Security Lead or an authorised delegate.

Logging and monitoring configuration, including retention and access to logging tools, shall be reviewed at least annually or when significant platform changes occur.

## Step 3 – Event Review

Significant events shall be reviewed to determine:

- Operational impact.
- Security impact.
- Root cause where practicable.
- Required corrective actions.

## Step 4 – Escalation

Where required, significant operational or security events shall be escalated through the established incident management process.

Appropriate technical or administrative actions shall be initiated based on the nature of the event.

## Step 5 – Record Maintenance

Log review activities and significant observations shall be documented in accordance with organisational procedures.

Operational records shall be retained to support monitoring, troubleshooting and audit requirements.

Minimum log retention shall be:

- Security, access and audit logs: 7 years.
- Operational troubleshooting logs: 90 days.
- Incident-related logs and extracts: retained with the applicable incident records.

The logging system shall be configured as an ingestion-only logging destination for routine operations. Authorised users may ingest, search and review logs, but the logging system does not provide a routine facility for users to delete ingested logs.

# 5. Documentation and Evidence

Supporting operational records, system logs and other technical evidence may be maintained, as appropriate, in accordance with operational requirements.

Evidence may include:

- Log review notes or monitoring observations.
- Alert investigation records for significant events.
- Incident record references where log observations are escalated.
- Logging configuration or retention evidence.
- Access review evidence for logging and monitoring tools.

# 6. Compliance

Compliance with this procedure is supported through documented operational records and implementation evidence maintained in accordance with organisational procedures.

Logging and monitoring activities shall support applicable GSTN operational, security and audit requirements.
