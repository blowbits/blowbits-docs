# 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the incident response framework for the Blowbits Solutions LLP GST Suvidha Provider (GSP) platform.

The objective of this policy is to ensure that information security incidents affecting the GSP platform are identified, reported, assessed, managed and resolved through structured operational processes to minimise operational impact and support the continued delivery of GSP services.

# 2. Scope

This policy applies to information security incidents affecting applications, APIs, infrastructure, networks, operational processes, taxpayer information and supporting services associated with the Blowbits Solutions LLP GSP platform.

Supporting procedures, role definitions, contact details, incident records and implementation evidence are maintained separately.

# 3. Incident Response Framework

Information security incidents are managed through structured operational processes.

The framework is established to:

- Support timely identification and reporting of security incidents.
- Assess and classify the operational and security impact of reported incidents.
- Escalate incidents to authorised roles according to severity and reporting requirements.
- Coordinate appropriate containment, investigation, recovery and corrective action activities.
- Notify GSTN Coordination Contact, CERT-In Reporting Channel, customers or other external communication points where applicable requirements apply.
- Maintain records of incident handling activities, decisions, communications and closure.
- Promote continual improvement through post-incident review.

# 4. Incident Response Principles

The GSP platform operates according to the following principles:

- Information security incidents are reported through established operational channels.
- Incidents are evaluated according to their operational impact, security impact and regulatory relevance.
- Appropriate containment measures are implemented to minimise service disruption and further exposure.
- Recovery activities are coordinated through authorised operational procedures.
- Incident handling activities are documented and retained.
- Significant incidents are subject to management oversight and closure review.
- Lessons identified during incident response support continual improvement.

# 5. Roles and Accountability

Incident response responsibilities are assigned to defined internal roles.

The Roles, Responsibilities & Access Matrix defines role-level accountability, authority and authorised access for internal roles including the GSP Coordination Lead, Platform Operations Lead, Information Security Lead and GSP Governance Lead.

Current role assignments, internal contact information and external communication points are maintained in the Contact Directory.

# 6. Incident Management Process

Incident response activities are managed through the Incident Response Procedure.

The operational framework includes:

- Incident identification.
- Incident reporting.
- Assessment and classification.
- Escalation and notification.
- Containment and investigation.
- Recovery and service restoration.
- Corrective and preventive action.
- Post-incident review and closure.

# 7. Compliance

Compliance with this policy is supported through documented procedures, operational practices, incident records, communication records, review evidence and implementation evidence.

Detailed operational processes, role assignments and contact details are maintained separately to support the effective implementation of this policy.
