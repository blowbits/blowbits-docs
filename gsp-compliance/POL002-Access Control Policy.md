# 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the access control framework for the Blowbits Solutions LLP GST Suvidha Provider (GSP) platform.

The objective of this policy is to ensure that access to applications, systems, infrastructure and information assets is granted, managed, reviewed and revoked through controlled processes based on operational responsibilities, business requirements and the principle of least privilege.

# 2. Scope

This policy applies to all users, administrators, authorised applications, infrastructure components, service accounts and supporting systems associated with the operation and maintenance of the Blowbits Solutions LLP GSP platform.

Supporting procedures, role definitions, technical standards, access records, review records and implementation evidence are maintained separately.

# 3. Access Control Framework

Access to GSP resources is governed through controlled identification, authentication and authorisation processes.

The framework is established to:

- Ensure access is granted only to authorised users, applications and system processes.
- Restrict access according to assigned responsibilities and operational need.
- Enforce least privilege for user, application and administrative access.
- Protect systems and information from unauthorised access.
- Support periodic review of access permissions.
- Maintain accountability for access to information assets.
- Support timely modification or revocation of access when responsibilities change or access is no longer required.

# 4. Roles and Accountability

Access control responsibilities are assigned through defined internal roles and operational processes.

The Roles, Responsibilities & Access Matrix defines role-level accountability, authority and authorised access for internal roles.

Access control governance is supported by the following role responsibilities:

- The GSP Governance Lead approves privileged access, access exceptions and significant access control decisions where required.
- The Platform Operations Lead implements approved access provisioning, modification and revocation activities for platform and infrastructure resources.
- The Information Security Lead reviews privileged access, access control effectiveness and security concerns relating to access permissions.

# 5. Access Control Principles

The GSP platform operates according to the following principles:

- Every user, application and service account is assigned an appropriate identity before access is granted.
- Access permissions are limited to business and operational requirements.
- Access is granted according to least privilege and need-to-know principles.
- Administrative and privileged access is restricted to authorised personnel.
- Privileged access requires approval by the GSP Governance Lead or an authorised delegate.
- Authentication is completed before access to protected resources is permitted.
- Multi-factor authentication (MFA) shall be used for privileged, administrative and externally accessible access where supported.
- Shared user accounts shall be avoided except where technically necessary and appropriately controlled.
- Access rights are reviewed periodically and updated when required.
- Access activities and administrative actions are logged where supported by the system or service.

# 6. Access Lifecycle Management

Access rights are managed throughout their operational lifecycle.

The lifecycle includes:

- Access request and business justification.
- Review and approval before provisioning.
- Provisioning of access according to approved responsibilities.
- Modification of access when operational responsibilities change.
- Periodic review of assigned privileges.
- Temporary suspension where required.
- Permanent revocation when access is no longer authorised.
- Prompt revocation when employment, engagement or operational need ends.

# 7. Privileged Access Management

Privileged access shall be limited to authorised roles and approved operational requirements.

Privileged access shall be:

- Approved before assignment.
- Restricted according to least privilege.
- Reviewed periodically.
- Revoked promptly when no longer required.
- Logged or monitored where supported by the system or service.

Where complete segregation of duties is not practical due to team size, compensating controls shall be applied through GSP Governance Lead approval, activity logging, periodic access review and documented oversight.

# 8. Exceptions

Exceptions to access control requirements shall be reviewed, approved and documented by the GSP Governance Lead where applicable.

Approved exceptions shall include a business justification, compensating controls where required and a review or expiry condition where practical.

# 9. Compliance

Compliance with this policy is supported through documented procedures, operational practices, access request records, approval records, access reviews, revocation evidence, privileged access records and implementation evidence.

Detailed operational processes and technical controls are maintained separately to support the effective implementation of this policy.

# 10. Review

This policy shall be reviewed annually or whenever significant operational, organisational, technical, regulatory or access control changes occur.
