# 1. Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) defines the process for planning, reviewing, approving, implementing and documenting changes affecting the Blowbits Solutions LLP GST Suvidha Provider (GSP) platform.

The objective of this procedure is to ensure that changes are implemented in a controlled manner while maintaining the security, stability and availability of GSP services.

# 2. Scope

This procedure applies to changes affecting applications, APIs, infrastructure, operating systems, security configurations, network components and operational processes supporting the Blowbits Solutions LLP GST Suvidha Provider (GSP) platform.

Operational records and supporting evidence generated during change activities are maintained separately.

# 3. Roles & Responsibilities

Change activities shall be performed by authorised internal roles defined in the Roles, Responsibilities & Access Matrix.

| Role | Change Management Responsibility |
| ---- | -------------------------------- |
| **Change Requestor** | Initiate and document the proposed change. |
| **Platform Operations Lead** | Coordinate impact assessment, implementation planning, deployment, rollback planning and validation. |
| **Information Security Lead** | Review security impact for changes affecting access, vulnerabilities, security controls, logging, sensitive information or external exposure. |
| **GSP Governance Lead** | Approve significant production changes, emergency changes, exceptions and changes with material operational, security or regulatory impact. |
| **GSP Coordination Lead** | Coordinate GSTN or authorised external communication where changes affect GSP operations or external coordination. |

# 4. Change Management Procedure

## Step 1 – Change Request

The proposed change shall be documented through the prescribed change request process.

The request shall include:

- Description of the proposed change.
- Business or operational justification.
- Systems affected.
- Change type and priority.
- Expected implementation schedule.
- Proposed implementer or responsible role.

Change types may include standard or routine changes, normal production changes, security changes, emergency changes and significant changes affecting availability, security, data integrity, regulatory commitments or external communication.

## Step 2 – Impact Assessment

The proposed change shall be evaluated to determine its potential impact on:

- Security.
- Platform availability.
- Existing functionality.
- Operational processes.
- Regulatory or contractual obligations.
- Data integrity or sensitive information.
- Rollback, recovery or backout requirements.

## Step 3 – Review & Approval

The change request shall be reviewed and approved in accordance with organisational procedures before implementation.

Significant production changes, emergency changes and changes with material operational, security or regulatory impact shall be approved by the GSP Governance Lead where required.

Emergency changes may be implemented on an expedited basis where immediate action is required to protect security, restore service or reduce material operational risk. Emergency changes shall be documented and reviewed after implementation.

## Step 4 – Implementation

Approved changes shall be implemented using controlled operational procedures.

Implementation activities may include:

- Implementation plan review.
- Configuration updates.
- Software deployment.
- Infrastructure modifications.
- Security configuration changes.
- Rollback or recovery preparation where applicable.
- Communication to relevant internal roles where required.

## Step 5 – Validation

Following implementation, the change shall be validated to confirm:

- Successful implementation.
- Expected functionality.
- No unacceptable operational impact.
- Continued availability of GSP services.
- Security controls remain effective where applicable.

Where necessary, rollback procedures shall be initiated.

Significant or emergency changes shall be reviewed after implementation where required to confirm outcome, issues encountered and follow-up actions.

## Step 6 – Closure

Following successful validation:

- Change records shall be updated.
- Supporting documentation shall be retained.
- Approval and validation evidence shall be retained where applicable.
- Follow-up actions shall be tracked where required.
- The change shall be formally closed.

# 5. Documentation and Evidence

Supporting operational records, system logs and other technical evidence may be maintained, as appropriate, in accordance with operational requirements.

Evidence may include:

- Change request or change ticket.
- Impact assessment.
- Approval record.
- Implementation plan or deployment record.
- Rollback or recovery plan where applicable.
- Validation or test evidence.
- Emergency change review record where applicable.
- Closure record.

# 6. Review

This procedure shall be reviewed annually or whenever significant changes occur to the GSP platform, change management process or operational responsibilities.

# 7. Compliance

Compliance with this procedure is supported through documented operational records and implementation evidence maintained in accordance with organisational procedures.
