# 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the framework for the management and protection of passwords, authentication credentials and security secrets used within the Blowbits Solutions LLP GST Suvidha Provider (GSP) platform.

The objective of this policy is to ensure that authentication information is protected throughout its lifecycle to prevent unauthorized access to applications, systems, infrastructure, APIs and information assets.

# 2. Scope

This policy applies to user passwords, administrative credentials, privileged credentials, application credentials, API credentials, service account credentials, system secrets, cryptographic material and other authentication information associated with the Blowbits Solutions LLP GSP platform.

Supporting procedures, technical standards, credential records, review records and implementation evidence are maintained separately.

# 3. Roles and Accountability

Credential management responsibilities are assigned through defined internal roles and operational processes.

The Roles, Responsibilities & Access Matrix defines role-level accountability, authority and authorised access for internal roles.

Credential management governance is supported by the following role responsibilities:

- The GSP Governance Lead approves credential management exceptions and significant privileged credential decisions where required.
- The Platform Operations Lead implements approved credential provisioning, storage, replacement and revocation activities for platform and infrastructure resources.
- The Information Security Lead reviews credential protection controls, privileged credential usage, suspected credential compromise and credential-related security concerns.

# 4. Credential Management Framework

Authentication credentials are managed through controlled processes designed to protect their confidentiality, integrity and availability.

The framework is established to:

- Ensure secure creation and provisioning of authentication credentials.
- Protect credentials during storage, transmission and operational use.
- Restrict credential access to authorised users, applications and system processes.
- Support periodic review of credential usage and privileged credential access.
- Enable rotation, replacement or revocation of credentials when required.
- Prevent unauthorised disclosure, hardcoding or plaintext storage of sensitive credentials.

# 5. Password & Credential Principles

The GSP platform operates according to the following principles:

- Authentication credentials are treated as confidential information.
- Passwords and sensitive credentials are protected using appropriate security controls.
- Plaintext storage of passwords and sensitive credentials is prohibited.
- Sensitive credentials shall be encrypted or stored in approved secure secret storage where supported.
- Credentials shall not be hardcoded in application source code, scripts, configuration repositories or client-side components.
- Credential access is restricted according to operational responsibilities and least privilege.
- Multi-factor authentication (MFA) shall be used for privileged, administrative and externally accessible access where supported.
- API credentials, application credentials and service account credentials shall be uniquely identifiable where practical.
- Credentials are rotated, replaced or revoked when compromise is suspected or confirmed, when operational responsibility changes, or when security or operational requirements require it.
- Credential management activities are supported through operational monitoring, logging or review where supported by the system or service.

# 6. Credential Lifecycle Management

Authentication credentials are managed throughout their operational lifecycle.

The lifecycle includes:

- Credential request and approval.
- Credential creation or generation.
- Secure storage.
- Controlled distribution or assignment.
- Authorised usage.
- Periodic review.
- Rotation or replacement when required.
- Revocation and secure disposal.

# 7. Exceptions

Exceptions to credential management requirements shall be reviewed, approved and documented by the GSP Governance Lead where applicable.

Approved exceptions shall include a business justification, compensating controls where required and a review or expiry condition where practical.

# 8. Compliance

Compliance with this policy is supported through documented procedures, operational practices, credential records, approval records, access reviews, rotation or replacement evidence, revocation evidence and implementation evidence.

Detailed operational processes and technical controls are maintained separately to support the effective implementation of this policy.

# 9. Review

This policy shall be reviewed annually or whenever significant operational, organisational, technical, regulatory or credential management changes occur.
